Caesar

Classic Caesar Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 lime wedge
  • 1 tablespoon celery salt, or as needed
  • ice cubes, as needed
  • 1 fluid ounce vodka
  • 1 dash Worcestershire sauce, or to taste
  • 1 dash hot pepper sauce (such as Tabasco®), or to taste
  • 8 fluid ounces tomato and clam juice cocktail (such as Clamato®)
  • 1 celery stick

Method

  1. Wet the rim of a cocktail glass with lime wedge; set lime wedge aside for garnish.
  2. Place celery salt in a small dish; press the rim of the glass into salt to coat. Add ice to the glass.
  3. Pour vodka, Worcestershire sauce, and hot pepper sauce over ice; top with tomato-clam juice.
  4. Garnish with lime wedge and celery stick. Serve with a straw.

Caesar History

The Caesar, or the Bloody Caesar, is considered Canada's national cocktail. It was invented by Walter Chell in Calgary, Alberta in 1969 after he was asked to craft a cocktail for the opening of an Italian restaurant at the Calgary Inn. Drawing inspiration from spaghetti alle vongole, a pasta dish made with clams, Chell created the Caesar using clam and tomato juice. IThe drink quickly became a favourite around Calgary before spreading to the rest of Canada, but its populatiry has not reached significantly beyond Canada.
