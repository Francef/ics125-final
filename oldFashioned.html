Old Fashioned

Classic Old-Fashioned Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 3 dashes Angostura bitters
  • 1 teaspoon water
  • 2 ounces bourbon
  • Garnish: orange peel

Method

  1. Add the sugar and bitters to a rocks glass, then add the water, and stir until the sugar is nearly dissolved.
  2. Fill the glass with large ice cubes, add the bourbon, and gently stir to combine.
  3. Express the oil of an orange peel over the glass, then drop in.

Old-Fashioned History

The old-fashioned is widely considered one of the oldest cocktails ever invented, if not the first. Its name is said to come from ordering cocktails "the old-fashioned" way, and has been written about since the 1800's. The first mention of anything close to the old-fashioned comes from Jerry Thomas' Bartenders Guide: How To Mix Drinks published in 1862, but this recipe uses gin with no mention of whiskey. The birth of the old-fashioned is credited to a private social club in Louisville, Kentucky called The Pendennis Club. The bartender of the club, James E. Pepper, was said to have invented the drink here before bringing it to the upper echelons of New York City.
