Mojito

Classic Mojito Recipe

Ingredients

  • 3-4 mint leaves
  • 15 mL simple syrup
  • 60 mL white rum
  • 20 mL lime juice
  • Club soda, to desired potency

Method

  1. In a glass, muddle the mint with the syrup until slightly bruised.
  2. Pour lime juice, rum, and ice into glass.
  3. Pour club soda overtop and stir until the syrup dissolves.
  4. ¡Salud!

Mojito History

The Mojito is widely known as the most famous drink that originates from Cuba. But did you know that there is no reference to the drink by name until 1932 when Jose Albeal penned “Sloppy Joe’s Bar Cocktails Manual”? There is no definitive year of inception of the tasty drink, but it’s close relative was a cocktail named El Draque. This drink was composed of nearly the same ingredients, save white rum, which was substituted with aguardiente, a cane sugar spirit that predates rum. El Draque, named after Sir Francis Drake, dates back to the 16th-century. So in a way when you are drinking a mojito you are sipping a little bit of Cuba’s history.
